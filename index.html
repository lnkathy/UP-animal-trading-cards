Flying Fish

Flying fish are ray-finned fish with highly modified pectoral fins. They propel themselves out of and glide on the water

  • Scientific Name: Exocoetidae
  • Average Length: 7-12 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 5 years
  • Habitat: Tropical & temperate marine

Flying fish propel themselves out of the water at speeds of more than 35 miles (56 kilometers) an hour and glide for up to 650 feet (200 meters). They can be up to 18 inches (45 centimeters) long. Flying fish eat a variety of foods, small crustaceans, and plankton.
